About the Lab

Who We Are

The EET-GS Lab is a global research initiative dedicated to advancing equitable, sustainable, and scientifically rigorous pathways for the energy transition across the Global South.

We generate knowledge, tools, and evidence that support emerging economies as they move toward clean, affordable, and resilient power systems—without compromising developmental priorities.

Our work integrates energy equity, distributional justice, and socio-economic impacts to ensure that decarbonization strategies enhance livelihoods, improve access, and contribute to inclusive development.

Research Programs

Flagship Research Programs

We co-design research with policy partners, utilities, and community leaders to deliver actionable insights.

Active

Equitable Grid Expansion Accelerator

Mapping last-mile electrification priorities and affordability metrics to ensure reliable access for rural and peri-urban communities. Develops open-source spatial planning tools and community-level affordability indices.

Active

Regional Renewable Integration Pathways

Long-term scenario planning for solar, wind, storage, and transmission build-out with a focus on reliability and cost-effectiveness, using PyPSA-based capacity expansion models calibrated for developing country contexts.

In Development

Just Transition Workforce Observatory

Tracking employment shifts, reskilling needs, and policy levers to protect vulnerable workers in the energy transition. Combines labor market data with energy system modeling to quantify job transitions.

Active

Demand-Side Flexibility Lab

Evaluating electrification, efficiency, and behavioral interventions to manage peak load and improve system resilience. Focuses on cooking, cooling, and mobility electrification in South and Southeast Asia.

Partnered

Policy and Financing Clinics

Evidence-based recommendations for national climate plans, regulatory reforms, and sustainable investment strategies, delivered through direct engagement with energy ministries and development finance institutions.

Active

Community Energy Equity Dashboard

Open-source data platform tracking equity indicators, subsidy impacts, and energy access progress. Designed for use by civil society organizations, regulators, and researchers across the Global South.
